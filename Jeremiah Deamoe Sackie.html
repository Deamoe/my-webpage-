A SHORT LIFE STORY YOU MUST KNOW

When I was much younger, one of my dreams was to complete my tertiary education in the city. I grew up in a small, beautiful rural community that was surrounded by farmland where I completed my secondary education. Due to my family's financial incapability, they struggled to pay for my tuition, including the tuition of my siblings.

Upon completion of my secondary education in 2016, I started to strategize a befitting plan as to how to relocate to the city, considering my parents’ financial standings. Six months had elapsed, yet, I couldn’t find my way out.

Therefore, I started to render my services as a teacher at an elementary school within my community. Through that, I was able to generate some cash that I used to transport myself in the city.

The city is full of influential and positive-minded people that could be a great help to my journey. When I arrived in the city, I had no one to accommodate me. This was one of the most challenging moments I have faced in my life at a young age.

However, I was never deterred. Fortunately for me, a friend who I once helped to get admission during our junior year in High School called me. We had a brief conversation.

I explained my situation to him and he gave me his brother’s particulars. His brother offered me a one-bedroom that was in an unfinished building. He told me that I could stay as long as I wished. I was excited although the place wasn’t fancy. For years plus, I suffered to sustain myself and I had to endure the bites of mosquitoes every night. Many nights I went to bed with an empty stomach.

As time went by, I started to become accustomed to city life. I began to practise journalism. I was so thirsty for success that I used to sleep on the floor at a radio station (FARBRIC RADIO 101.1 FM) in order to use their internet service so that I could explore the internet for opportunities.

Consequently, I was able to travel to Addis Ababa, Ethiopia, where I attended the Young African Thinkers Convention (YATC2018). I met inspiring and like-minded, positive-driven youth from across Africa. Upon my return, I got many opportunities that enabled me to live a much preferable life in the city.

I have learned so many things from this experience; one of those is to be steadfast in pursuit of our goals. Today, I am the only person in my family to have reached this far, living in the city, and schooling in a techanical university "Starz College of Science and Technology", studying Bachelor of Information Technology(BIT).
